BY EMMANUEL ROMERO L.A. Cicero Richard Zare
Proffesor Richard Zare in his lab. Wheather it's the summer
grass that tickles your feet or the red Boardeaux smacking on
your palate, nearly every part of the world around you carries
special chemical markers. These markers, called isotopes, can
tell scientist where the molecules that compose a substance
are form, where they traveled and what happened to them
along the way. But doing these analyses has been complex
and costly. Now Stanford chemists have developed a new
method to make isotopic analysis easier and less expensive.
